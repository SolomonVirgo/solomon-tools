Phase 1 · Months 1–2
Business foundation
Name · Legal · Positioning · Pricing
0/5
1
Choose your business name
Shortlist 3 names using the Virgo brand. Strong options: Virgo Manufacturing Solutions, Virgo NPI Consulting, or Virgo Product Commercialization. Check availability at bcregistrynames.gov.bc.ca. Keep it broad enough to cover future services including training and digital products.
Foundation1 evening · ~1.5 hrs
2
Register as a sole proprietor in BC
Register your business name with BC Registry Services online. Sole proprietor is the fastest and cheapest structure — you can incorporate later when revenue justifies it. Cost is approximately $50–80. No lawyer needed at this stage. Keep your registration certificate — you'll need it to open your business bank account.
Foundation1 evening · ~1 hr online
3
Open a dedicated business bank account
Keep business income completely separate from personal finances from day one. Most major Canadian banks offer free or low-cost small business accounts. Bring your BC registration certificate. This makes tax time simple and looks professional when clients pay you.
Foundation1 hr · in-branch visit
4
Write your positioning statement
One sentence that describes what you do, who you serve, and what outcome you deliver. Starting point: "I help small and mid-size manufacturers reduce risk and accelerate product launches through expert NPI and manufacturing readiness consulting." Refine it until it flows naturally in conversation. This becomes the headline of everything you do.
Foundation1 evening · ~1 hr
5
Set your pricing structure
Establish three rates before any client conversation: Hourly ($125–150/hr for general consulting), Project-based ($2,500–5,000 for a Manufacturing Readiness Audit), Monthly retainer ($2,000–3,000/month for fractional engagements). Write these down and commit to them. Knowing your numbers before a conversation is the difference between confident and desperate.
Foundation1 evening · ~1 hr
Phase 2 · Months 2–4
Brand and online presence
LinkedIn · Website · Capability statement
0/4
6
Overhaul your LinkedIn profile for consulting
Update your headline from job title to value statement: "NPI & Manufacturing Readiness Consultant | Helping Manufacturers Launch Products Faster". Rewrite your About section to speak to potential clients, not employers — what problem do you solve, not where have you worked. Add consulting positioning to the Featured section. LinkedIn is your most powerful marketing asset right now.
Brand2 evenings · ~3 hrs total
7
Write and publish your first LinkedIn article
Suggested title: "10 Reasons New Product Launches Fail — And How to Avoid Them." Draw from your 30 years of real NPI experience across 6 industries. Aim for 700–900 words. This single post begins establishing you as a thought leader to the exact audience you want to reach. Use Claude to help structure and polish it — the ideas and experience are yours.
Brand2 evenings · ~2.5 hrs
8
Build a simple professional website
Use Wix, Squarespace, or Webflow. You need five pages: Home (positioning + services overview), About (your story and credentials), Services (detailed), Blog/Insights (for LinkedIn article reposts), and Contact. Domain suggestion: virgoMFGsolutions.com or virgoNPI.com (~$15/yr). Hosting ~$15–20/month. Clean and professional beats fancy and complex every time.
Brand3–4 evenings · ~6 hrs total
9
Create a one-page capability statement (PDF)
A professional PDF you can email or share with potential clients and referral contacts. Include: positioning statement, core service lines (NPI Consulting, Manufacturing Readiness, Technical Documentation), industries served, key credentials (30+ years, 100+ products launched, B.Eng. 1st Class Honours), and contact details. This is your business card for serious conversations.
Brand1 evening · ~2 hrs
Phase 3 · Months 4–7
Service portfolio and operations
Templates · Deliverables · Contracts · Invoicing
0/5
10
Document your NPI consulting service package
Write down exactly what a client gets when they hire you for NPI Consulting: what you assess, what you deliver, what a typical engagement looks like week by week, and what success looks like at the end. This becomes your proposal template. Think of your Bailey NPI Framework — that same structured thinking, adapted for external clients.
Portfolio2 evenings · ~3 hrs
11
Build 3 reusable deliverable templates
Create professional templates for: (1) Manufacturing Readiness Assessment checklist, (2) NPI Gate Review template (EVT/DVT/PVT), (3) PFMEA starter template. These become your working tools with clients and eventually your first digital products for sale. Use your existing Bailey documents as the base — strip out anything proprietary and build the general framework.
Portfolio3 evenings · ~5 hrs total
12
Write a consulting proposal template
Create a clean Word/PDF proposal you can customize per client in under an hour. Sections: Executive Summary, Understanding of Your Challenge, Scope of Work, Deliverables, Timeline, Investment (pricing), About Solomon Virgo, Terms and Conditions. Having this ready means you can respond to any opportunity within 24 hours — speed signals professionalism and wins work.
Portfolio1 evening · ~2 hrs
13
Set up invoicing and register for GST/HST
Use Wave (free) or FreshBooks (~$15/month) to send professional invoices. Register for a GST/HST number with CRA — required once you expect to exceed $30,000 in annual consulting revenue. A good problem to plan for now. Consult a bookkeeper for a one-time setup session (~$100–200) to establish your books correctly from the start.
Operations1 evening · ~1.5 hrs setup
14
Draft a consulting agreement
A 1–2 page contract covering: scope of work, payment terms (50% upfront recommended), IP ownership (your templates stay yours), confidentiality, and termination clause. Search "consulting agreement template Canada BC" — adapt a free template for your situation. Have a lawyer review it once (~$200–300). This protects you and signals professionalism to serious clients.
Operations1 evening · ~1.5 hrs
Phase 4 · Months 7–10
Market and build your network
Warm outreach · Content · Communities
0/5
15
Map your warm network — identify 20 key contacts
List 20 people who already know your work: former colleagues and managers from Eaton, Teledyne FLIR, Cadex, Vecima, OMNEX, Brooks Automation, Scientific Atlanta, Cable & Wireless. Supply chain contacts, OEM contacts, past collaborators. These are your first referral sources — people who already trust you. You don't pitch them. You reconnect genuinely and let them know you're consulting.
Marketing1 evening · ~1 hr
16
Send 5 warm outreach messages per month
Not pitches — genuine reconnections. Something like: "Hey [name], been a while. I've started a consulting practice focused on NPI and manufacturing readiness for manufacturers. Would love to catch up sometime." That's it. From 20 contacts over several months, 2–3 conversations will naturally turn into referrals or direct opportunities. Consistency matters far more than volume.
Marketing30 min/month · ongoing
17
Post one piece of LinkedIn content per week
Rotate between four content types: (1) a lesson from your career, (2) a manufacturing or NPI problem you've solved, (3) a tip or insight about DFM, PFMEA, production readiness, (4) a question that sparks discussion. Each post is 150–250 words. You're not trying to go viral — you're building credibility with the 50–100 people who will eventually refer or hire you. Use Claude to draft and refine.
Marketing30–45 min/week · ongoing
18
Join 2 manufacturing LinkedIn groups and engage
Search for: "Manufacturing Engineering," "NPI Engineering," "Medical Device Manufacturing," "Lean Manufacturing," "Industrial Automation." Engage authentically — answer questions, share genuine insights, add value to discussions. This expands your reach beyond existing connections and positions you as a contributing expert, not a job seeker or salesperson.
Marketing30 min setup + 15 min/week
19
Create a profile on one consulting platform
Register on Toptal, Expert360, or Kolabtree for technical consulting. These platforms connect experienced consultants with companies seeking short-term expertise without hiring full-time. Your 30+ years across 6 industries and B.Eng. 1st Class Honours will stand out. Don't rely on it as your only channel — treat it as a parallel pipeline that may deliver your first paid engagement.
Marketing1 evening · ~1.5 hrs
Phase 5 · Months 10–12
Land first client and build revenue
Discovery calls · First engagement · Testimonial · Digital products
0/5
20
Offer free 60-minute Manufacturing Readiness Calls
To warm prospects and new connections, offer a no-obligation discovery call where you assess one specific manufacturing or NPI challenge they have. In 60 minutes you will demonstrate more value than most engineers show in a month. This is your foot in the door. Many of these calls convert naturally to paid engagements — not because you sell, but because you've already helped.
Revenue1 hr per call · schedule as ready
21
Land and deliver your first paid engagement
Your first client does not need to be large. A 10-hour NPI assessment at $150/hr = $1,500. A small technical documentation project. A one-day Manufacturing Readiness Audit for $2,500. The goal is one paying client, excellent work delivered, and a testimonial earned. That first engagement is the proof of concept that opens every door after it. Aim for this by month 12.
RevenueTarget: before month 12
22
Request a written testimonial
After delivering value, ask your client: "Would you be willing to write 2–3 sentences about working together that I could use on my website?" Most satisfied clients say yes immediately. One strong, specific testimonial from a real company is worth more than any marketing material you could ever create. Put it on your website, LinkedIn, and capability statement the same day you receive it.
Revenue5 min ask · invaluable result
23
Launch your first digital product
Take one of your completed templates (Manufacturing Readiness Checklist or NPI Gate Review) and sell it on Gumroad or directly from your website for $29–79. Set it up in an evening. This creates passive income and attracts potential consulting clients — the people who buy your templates are the same people who may later hire you directly. Build once, sell repeatedly.
Revenue1 evening to publish
24
Year-end review — set Year 2 targets
At month 12: review what worked, what didn't, what your pipeline looks like, and what your total consulting revenue was. Set Year 2 goals — target 2 recurring clients at $2,000–2,500/month = $4,000–5,000/month. Begin planning a training workshop or online course. By Year 2 you should be firmly on the path to $75K+ annually from consulting alongside your Bailey salary.
